# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Tech recruiters, engineering hiring managers, and senior engineers at companies hiring for SWE, AI/ML, and SRE roles. They're mid-loop, reviewing many candidates on a desktop browser in a professional context. They need signal fast: can this person ship production-grade systems at scale? The portfolio must communicate technical credibility within seconds and hold up to scrutiny on detail.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Sourik Dutta, Software Engineer with 2+ years shipping distributed backend systems, LLM-as-a-service platforms, AIOps telemetry pipelines, and agentic RAG architectures at Insight Enterprises and Hanu Software. MS Computer Science, New York University. Open to new roles across SWE, Agentic AI, and SRE tracks.

Success: a recruiter reads the first fold and wants to reach out. A senior engineer scrolls through and finds specific technical depth that signals real production experience.

## Brand Personality

Ops-minded, technically authoritative, no-nonsense. The aesthetic is a live control room, not a portfolio. Military and terminal vocabulary throughout: BLUEPRINTS, SERVICE LOGS, THE SYSTEM, COMMAND CENTER. Dark, structured, signal-over-noise.

Three words: Authoritative. Precise. Operational.

## Anti-references

- Generic glassmorphism developer portfolios (blurred card grids, purple/teal gradients everywhere)
- Soft, pastel creative developer portfolios
- Over-animated scrollytelling portfolios that bury the work under visual noise
- Hero-metric SaaS templates (big stat block, gradient accent, "99.9% uptime" claims)
- Any design a recruiter would describe as "cute" or "artistic"
- Gradient text (background-clip: text with a gradient) — always looks cheap

## Design Principles

1. **Signal over noise** — every visual element competes with the content for attention. If a decoration isn't earning its place, it goes.
2. **Expertise shown, not claimed** — the language, structure, and specificity of the technical content signals seniority. Design amplifies it, never upstages it.
3. **Terminal native** — vocabulary and aesthetic come from command-line tools and production dashboards, not consumer apps or creative portfolios.
4. **Density is credibility** — thin, sparse layouts feel junior. The system matrix, experience logs, and project specs carry real operational depth.
5. **One voice throughout** — every section uses the same ops terminology and structural grammar. Nothing breaks character.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion supported via `prefers-reduced-motion` media query and `useReducedMotion` hook throughout. Color contrast: alabaster (#dde8f5) on graphite-100 (#07111f) passes AA at all sizes.
